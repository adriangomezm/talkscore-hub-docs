1. What Is TalkScore Hub?

TalkScore Hub is a web-based platform that monitors, analyzes, and reports on AI-conducted candidate assessments at scale. It serves as the quality control center for every AI interview powered by Talkpush's SAM (Smart Assessment Module) voice interviewer.

When SAM conducts a voice interview with a candidate, it produces a transcript, scores, and language proficiency data. TalkScore Hub ingests all of that data automatically and then:

  • Runs secondary AI analysis on every interview transcript to detect quality issues, agent errors, and potential bias
  • Presents real-time dashboards with assessment volume, scores, CEFR language levels, and completion rates
  • Generates alerts when quality thresholds are breached — such as dropout spikes, scoring inconsistencies, or bias flags
  • Provides an AI Assistant that answers natural-language questions about assessment data ("What was the average score for Alorica Philippines last week?")
  • Tracks AI costs so administrators can monitor usage across all clients
In One Sentence
TalkScore Hub is the quality assurance and analytics dashboard for every AI interview Talkpush conducts — it ensures assessments are fair, consistent, and high-quality across all clients and agents.

5.2 Agent Health

Quality Monitoring

Agent Health is the quality control center. It shows what's going wrong in AI interviews and where to focus attention.

Three Analysis Sections

Quality Flags

Every completed interview is analyzed by AI (Claude 3.5 Sonnet) for 25 quality flag types across three categories:

CategoryFlags DetectedExamples
Agent Quality (12 flags) Problems with how the AI interviewer behaved Robotic repetition, ignoring candidate responses, hallucinating information, language mismatch, premature question advancement
Bias & Fairness (9 flags) Potential discrimination or unfair treatment — all flagged as Critical Gender bias, age bias, accent discrimination, name-based bias, cultural bias, disability bias
Candidate Behavior (4 flags) Notable candidate reactions during the interview Candidate frustration, confusion, disengagement, inappropriate behavior

Each flag includes: severity level, an explanation of what happened, evidence quoted from the transcript, and a timestamp.

Dropout Analysis

Shows where and why candidates are abandoning interviews before completion:

  • Stage Funnel — visual showing which interview stage candidates drop off at (introduction, question 1, question 2, etc.)
  • Dropout Reasons — AI-categorized reasons grouped with transcript excerpts
  • AI Analysis — click any dropout reason to get an AI-written analysis with root cause, impact summary, and recommended fixes

Stage Deep Dive

Per-stage analysis of the interview funnel:

  • How many candidates reached each stage
  • Average duration per stage
  • Dropout count and reasons per stage
  • Quality flags that occur at each stage
  • Transcript excerpts from problematic interactions

5.3 Score Calibration

Scoring Consistency

Score Calibration answers: "Are our AI agents scoring candidates consistently and fairly?"

What It Shows

  • Company Average Score — the mean score across all completed assessments
  • Standard Deviation — how spread out scores are (lower = more consistent)
  • Per-Agent Breakdown — each AI agent's average score, standard deviation, call count, min/max score. Flags agents that deviate significantly from the company mean.
  • Wave Calibration — groups assessments into time periods ("waves") to show how scores trend over time. Detects score drift — when an agent starts scoring higher or lower than usual.

Outcome Analysis

This powerful sub-feature compares assessment scores against actual hiring outcomes:

  • Hired vs. Rejected — do candidates who score higher actually get hired more often?
  • Score by Pipeline Stage — average score for candidates at each Talkpush status (Applied, Interviewed, Offered, Hired, Rejected)
  • CEFR by Outcome — do language proficiency levels correlate with hiring decisions?
  • Soft Skills by Outcome — which soft skill dimensions predict success?

6. How TalkScore Scoring Works

TalkScore evaluates spoken English across five dimensions using AI and speech analysis models. The scoring is holistic — not a simple penalty system.

Pronunciation

Evaluates clarity and correctness of each phoneme (sound). Scale: 0–100 per word. Accents are NOT penalized unless they affect intelligibility.

Fluency

Measures speech rate (syllables/second), pause frequency, pause duration, and run length between pauses. Natural flow = high score.

Grammar

Checks accuracy AND complexity of sentence structures. Simple-but-correct may score lower than complex-with-minor-errors. Trained on human-rated samples.

Vocabulary

Measures lexical diversity, word sophistication, word specificity, and use of collocations/idioms. Rewards precise, varied word choice.

Coherence

Evaluates use of connective words (because, however, therefore), logical flow, lexical density, and pronoun clarity. Rewards organized, easy-to-follow responses.

Overall Score

Composite AI evaluation aligned to IELTS-style band scale (0–10) and CEFR levels (A1–C2). Not a simple average — learned from human examiner ratings.

CEFR Level Mapping

CEFR LevelProficiency LabelWhat It Means
C1, C2ExpertNear-native fluency, complex language with few errors
B2High ProficiencyCan handle complex topics, clear and detailed speech
B1Moderate ProficiencyCan deal with most everyday situations, some errors
A1, A2Low ProficiencyBasic phrases only, limited communication ability

Job Matching Score

Separate from language scoring, TalkScore also produces a Job Matching Score (1–5) that evaluates how well the candidate fits the specific role based on their interview responses, extracted data (education, experience, availability), and AI-generated insights.

Key Point for Support
TalkScore does NOT simply subtract points for each mistake. It uses machine learning trained on human evaluators. A speaker who makes a few grammar errors but uses complex, varied sentences may score higher than someone who speaks simply but error-free. The scoring mirrors what a trained human interviewer would assess.

7. Alert System — Deep Dive

The alert system runs automatically after every incoming assessment. When thresholds are breached, alerts are generated and optionally sent via email.

Alert Lifecycle

  1. Detection — system checks all 9 rules against recent data (within the configured lookback period)
  2. Deduplication — if the same alert was generated in the last 24 hours, it's not duplicated
  3. Notification — critical/high alerts trigger email to subscribed users
  4. Review — user sees alert badge on "AI Insights" in sidebar, reviews and investigates
  5. Resolution — user marks as read or dismisses; can navigate to Agent Health for deeper analysis

Alert Severity Guide

SeverityWhen It FiresRecommended Action
Info Metrics show minor deviation from normal Monitor — no immediate action needed
Warning Threshold exceeded, potential quality issue Investigate within 24-48 hours. Check affected agents/campaigns.
Critical Bias flags detected, or critical thresholds breached Investigate immediately. May require disabling the affected agent or escalating to the client.

10. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How quickly does data appear after an assessment completes?

Assessment data appears within seconds of the webhook being received. AI analysis (quality flags and stage analysis) completes within 30–60 seconds after that.

Q: Can I see the actual conversation transcript?

Yes. Every assessment record includes the full transcript with timestamps, showing both the AI agent's questions and the candidate's responses. Access it from the Assessment Detail or Candidate Report view.

Q: What does "Not Taken" status mean?

"Not Taken" means the candidate was invited to take the assessment but never started it — essentially a no-show. "Unfinished" means they started but dropped out before completing all questions.

Q: Can I customize what the AI checks for?

Alert thresholds are fully configurable per company (e.g., change the dropout spike threshold from 30% to 20%). The 25 quality flag types are system-defined and cannot be changed, but they cover the most important quality dimensions.

Q: Does TalkScore Hub penalize candidates for having an accent?

No. TalkScore's pronunciation scoring measures intelligibility — how easily the speaker can be understood — not accent. A thick accent with clear pronunciation can score just as well as native pronunciation. Bias flag detection specifically monitors for accent discrimination.

Q: Can I export reports?

Yes. Individual candidate reports can be exported as PDF directly from the browser. Dashboard data can be viewed and shared by granting users access to the platform.

Q: How is data security handled?

All data is company-isolated (multi-tenant). Authentication uses Google OAuth with server-side session tokens. Webhooks are verified using HMAC SHA-256 signatures. API endpoints require session or API key authentication.

Q: What if I see a bias flag? What should I do?

Bias flags are always marked as critical. Review the flag's explanation and evidence (transcript excerpt). If confirmed, escalate to the product team — the AI agent's prompt or configuration may need adjustment. Document the finding for the client if their assessments were affected.

Q: Can I ask the AI Assistant about data across multiple clients?

The AI Assistant operates within the context of the currently selected client. To compare across clients, you would need to switch between clients or ask a platform admin to run cross-client queries.
